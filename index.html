About Nature-Based Solutions

Nature-Based Solutions (NBS) are innovative approaches to urban development that work with nature to address societal challenges, providing environmental, social, and economic benefits. These solutions are inspired by, supported by, or copied from nature, and aim to help societies address environmental, social, and economic challenges sustainably.

Types of Nature-Based Solutions

  • Urban Trees and Forests
  • Green Roofs and Walls
  • Rain Gardens and Bioswales
  • Permeable Pavements
  • Urban Wetlands
  • Pollinator Gardens
  • Urban Agriculture

Benefits of Nature-Based Solutions

  • Climate Change Mitigation and Adaptation
  • Improved Water Management
  • Enhanced Air Quality
  • Increased Biodiversity
  • Urban Heat Island Effect Reduction
  • Improved Public Health and Well-being
  • Economic Benefits and Job Creation

Implementation Strategies

Implementing Nature-Based Solutions requires a holistic approach:

  1. Assessment of local challenges and opportunities
  2. Stakeholder engagement and participatory planning
  3. Integration with existing urban planning frameworks
  4. Securing funding and resources
  5. Design and construction
  6. Monitoring and evaluation
  7. Maintenance and adaptive management

Technical Aspects

Urban Trees

Species selection is crucial for maximizing benefits. Factors to consider include:

  • Climate adaptability
  • Pollution tolerance
  • Growth rate and mature size
  • Root system characteristics
  • Maintenance requirements

Green Roofs

Key technical considerations include:

  • Structural load capacity of the building
  • Waterproofing and drainage systems
  • Substrate composition and depth
  • Plant selection for harsh rooftop conditions
  • Irrigation and maintenance access

Permeable Pavements

Important technical aspects:

  • Subgrade preparation and compaction
  • Selection of appropriate aggregate layers
  • Permeable surface material choice (e.g., porous asphalt, permeable interlocking concrete pavers)
  • Design for local hydrological conditions
  • Maintenance to prevent clogging
